# Project PROMIS Briefing

## Overview
- **Core claim:** DOJ stole Enhanced PROMIS from Inslaw in 1983 and, with attorney general Edwin Meese and deputy AG Lowell Jensen, handed the proprietary software to Earl Brian’s Hadron/InfoTech network, which routed a back-doored version to Israeli handlers and U.S. defense labs via Robert Maxwell’s Pergamon/Information on Demand. 
- **Supporting evidence:** Michael Riconosciuto’s affidavit (Last Circle ch.2/6) shows the Wackenhut/Cabazon modification; the House Judiciary Report 102-857 transcript (InslawReportText.txt) lays out the theft allegations; the FBI Maxwell counterintelligence packet (Maxwell_FBI.txt) documents Case AQ 105 C-3262 citing the Trojan horse; Reagan Library topic guide (reagan_inslaw.txt) lists FG006/JL007/CFOA casefiles for Meese/Inslaw memos.

## Timeline (with citations)
1. **1982** – DOJ contracts with Inslaw for PROMIS installs across 94 U.S. attorneys’ offices while encouraging privately funded enhancements; Inslaw notes the Department recognized its proprietary enhancements but later withheld payments (House report pp.3534-3558).
2. **1983** – DOJ abruptly cancels part of the contract, cuts payments, and pushes Inslaw into bankruptcy while Meese/Jensen allegedly conspire to reassign the software (House report pp.3554-3626).
3. **1983-84** – Earl Brian hands a copy of PROMIS to Michael Riconosciuto at Cabazon; Riconosciuto’s affidavit (Last Circle ch.2/6) says he modified the code to be used globally and DOJ procurement official Peter Videnieks visited the project.
4. **1984-91** – Robert Maxwell’s Pergamon/Pergamon subsidiary sells the back-doored PROMIS to U.S. defense installations including Sandia, causing FBI Albuquerque to open foreign counterintelligence Case AQ 105 C-3262 because Israeli intelligence could use the Trojan (Maxwell_FBI.txt, pp.326-369).
5. **1992-93** – House Report 102-857 documents the conspiracy, with Meese/Jensen and Ari Ben-Menashe linking DOJ theft to Israeli intel operations; Inslaw adds Bua report material (InslawReportText.txt pp.3590-3925).
6. **1994** – FBI releases Maxwell FOIA documents to Inslaw, reinforcing the Israeli/DOJ partnership (Maxwell_FBI.txt pp.344-363).
7. **Reagan Library files (referenced 2026)** – topic guide lists FG006-01, FG017, JL002, JL007, and CFOA 853 as the relevant collections for the Meese/Inslaw correspondence and Bua report exhibits, giving us the archival handles for follow-up.

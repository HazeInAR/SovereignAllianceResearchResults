## Summary
- Times Higher Education mined DOJ Epstein files and found Epstein kept close tabs on academia long after his 2008 plea, circulating articles about research breakthroughs and even university rankings.
- August 2015: Epstein dined at Baume Restaurant with Elon Musk, Mark Zuckerberg, Peter Thiel, MIT neuroscientist Ed Boyden, and former MIT Media Lab leaders Joi Ito and Desiree Dudley—several years after he had been convicted of soliciting a minor for prostitution.
- Epstein cultivated scholar relationships via intermediaries such as John Brockman (Edge Foundation) and his network of agents, ensuring he remained embedded in elite intellectual circles despite his criminal record.
- The article notes appearances in the files are not evidence of wrongdoing; many academics denied improper ties. Epstein’s moves instead signal he was shaping a narrative of being a patron of cutting-edge science.
- Additional DOJ highlights include Geoffrey West at the Santa Fe Institute, Stephen Kosslyn’s jail visits, Peter Fenwick’s Highlands pitch, Lisa Randall flying to Little St. James, Leon Botstein signing “Miss you,” Woody Allen’s daughter entering Bard, Mortimer Zuckerman, Deepak Chopra, Noam Chomsky, and Marci Hamilton framing him as an ego-driven hanger-on who believed academia validated him.
- Files quote academics describing Epstein as a peer who feigned intellectual curiosity—Nicholas Christakis, Nathan Wolfe (and his “hottie interns”), and others were dragged into lengthy theory conversations while he used Columbia and other universities to place girlfriends and influence careers; Hamilton sees the “chummy” demeanor as another elite wrapper for his trafficking.
- Later sections highlight that Joi Ito moved to Chiba Institute of Technology post-2019, Christian Stohler tried connecting via LinkedIn, Harvard visits were scheduled with Andrew Strominger and Daniel Lieberman (Lieberman later denied the meeting), Stephen Greenblatt discussed workshops with Lesley Groff, and Ian Osborne (the “fixer”) tried to book Niall Ferguson—showing Epstein kept pulling in academics and fixers long after his conviction.
- Further emails describe Osborne trying to meet Ferguson in NYC, Ferguson and other scholars slated for Thiel’s 2013 Dialog Retreat, Martin Nowak appearing 8,000+ times in the files and brokering dinners with Chomsky/Summers, and Epstein’s financial pushes (Joscha Bach in AI, Antonio Damasio for USC robotics, Mark Tramo’s $500K request) that tied him to science funding while those donors later expressed regret.
- Experts like Shamus Khan and Joanna Bryson now see Epstein’s academic cache as status-driven: institutions like Harvard, MIT, Columbia attracted him for influence as much as money. Bryson notes that wealth becomes a magnet so intellects cluster in “the cool crowd,” and Zuckerman hosted a 2013 dinner with Columbia’s Lee Bollinger, who believes Epstein used the academics as a foundational power base for his “nefarious ends.”

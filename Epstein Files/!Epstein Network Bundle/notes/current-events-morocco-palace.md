# Current Events: Epstein’s Unfinished Moroccan Palace Purchase
**Source:** https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2026/2/24/how-epstein-tried-to-buy-a-moroccan-palace-months-before-his-death
**Published:** February 24, 2026 (Al Jazeera)

## Summary
- DOJ documents show that in early 2019 Jeffrey Epstein was negotiating to acquire the Bin Ennakhil estate outside Marrakesh—a 4.6-hectare palace with dozens of marble fountains—through an offshore structure involving a BVI trust and a Liechtenstein company called “The Haze Trust.” Karyna Shuliak managed the deal on his behalf.
- Deutsche Bank rejected a March 13 wire tied to Epstein. He opened new accounts at Charles Schwab that April (including one for Southern Trust, the buyer), and a June 26 transfer of €11.15 million to the Moroccan realtor was reversed at Schwab’s request; two days before his arrest a second $14.95 million instruction (signed by Epstein) was submitted but cancelled when the prior funds hadn’t yet returned.
- Internal DOJ/SAR notes show Schwab flagged the wires amid growing media scrutiny and Epstein’s flight-risk status. By July his financial pathways were closing just as prosecutors seized devices and evidence in New York, Florida, and the US Virgin Islands—two days after the second wire request he was arrested at Teterboro.
- The article also reminds that Morocco featured in Epstein’s network long before 2019 (e.g., recruiter Daniel Siad circulated Moroccan girls in 2002) and frames the palace attempt as part of the closing loop on his wealth as banks tightened compliance before his arrest and death.

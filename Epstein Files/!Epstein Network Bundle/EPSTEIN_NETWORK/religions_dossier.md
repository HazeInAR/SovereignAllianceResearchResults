## Televangelists & Mega Church Network
- **Kenneth Copeland / Kenneth Copeland Ministries (KCM)** — A Word of Faith prosperity preacher whose Eagle Mountain International Church sits on the former Marine Corps Air Station Eagle Mountain Lake outside Fort Worth, Texas. The complex includes the Kenneth Copeland Airport, a private airstrip used to shuttle donors and supporters, and it is the same regional air corridor that connects to the Zorro Ranch logistics route and some of the Dallas/Fort Worth-based aviation nodes we already monitor. Copeland runs the *Believer's Voice of Victory* broadcast, the Victory Channel, and annual conventions that attract six-figure gifts; he explicitly preaches that giving unlocks material blessing, which saturates the same wealth-and-faith narrative used by elite social engineers to mobilize audiences. (Sources: public biographies, KCM filings, [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kenneth_Copeland)).
- **Joel Osteen / Lakewood Church** — Houston-based megachurch whose televised services and book publishing empire preach a sanitized prosperity gospel. Osteen’s organization is a media franchise comparable to Copeland’s and channels massive donations while projecting a message of personal self-empowerment that dovetails with elite consumer culture. Osteen frequently shares stages or co-hosts events with other mega-preachers, which provides a social network for American business/political elites looking for moral cover.
- **700 Club / Pat Robertson / CBN** — The Christian Broadcasting Network is a global media network that amplifies messages about national identity, geopolitics, and divine favor for Western elites. Its fundraising apparatus (telethons, donor clubs, and international ministries) resembles the media spin operations we track in other sectors; the 700 Club format also interoperates with the same television infrastructure (TBN, Victory Channel, Daystar) that the Copeland/Osteen networks use to saturate the same viewer base.
- **Jim and Tammy Faye Bakker** — Once the faces of televangelism’s 1980s financial scandals, later probation and activism, and now retroactive symbols for the same prosperity/protection narrative. Their long record of high-profile fundraising, criminal convictions, and return to spiritual branding (via new TV channels and streaming) is a cautionary mirror for the new generation of mega-preachers who operate with less scrutiny.
- **Billy Graham & Legacy Evangelical Circuits** — Graham’s international crusades carved the blueprint for this elite-soaked spiritual apparatus; his vast donor/resonance network feeds into modern mega churches (Osteen, Copeland, TBN) which keep his associations (prayer breakfasts, international summits, and executive-level offerings) alive.

**Key threads**
- `Urban Moving Systems` (Weehawken/Jersey City, NJ) was the employer of the five men who became known as the “Dancing Israelis.” The DOJ’s `dancing-israelis` archive (Sections 1–5) records agents tracing their van, their celebratory filming, and their employer’s office at 312 Pavonia Ave #1. ## Outstanding questions & why the FBI may have stayed quiet
- **Hijacker link:** the FBI notebooks and Classic International interviews mention **one of the nineteen suspects** using that mover right before the attack, but the publicly shared documents do not name the specific hijacker—only that Classic International’s number was found in the seized notebook. (Source: Archive.org text with the Classic International mention.)
- **Company lifespans:** Urban Moving Systems existed at least through 2001 (photos, directories). Classic International Movers is referenced in the same immediate aftermath, so both were operational before 9/11. LLC and corporate filings are required in NJ/NY when those companies formed; the NJ Division of Revenue archives and the later NY LLC filings for 24/7 Anyday show the Suters kept up with those requirements—at least on paper. (Sources: Yelp/MapQuest, NY SOS filing via the blog post.)
- **Suter’s return:** Because Suter disappeared shortly after the arrests and then allegedly resumed life under new company names, the public record stops: there is no DOJ press release saying the FBI recaptured him, nor is there a follow-up document about 24/7 Anyday. That silence (detention → deportation → no trial) is why the conspiracy thread persists. Without a formal statement that the FBI cleared or re-interviewed the new identity, watchers conclude he was never fully held accountable. (Source: absence of DOJ updates; blog posts noting there was no public FBI remark.)

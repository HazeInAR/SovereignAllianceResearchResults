# "Dancing Israelis" — 9/11 Arrest Records & Sources

**Core story**
- On September 11, 2001 five employees of Urban Moving Systems were detained in New Jersey after reports said they were videotaping the attacks and acting unusually cheerful. Authorities initially suspected the men might be Mossad agents because of their behavior, travel documents, and contacts, but the FBI ultimately cleared them and deported most of the group.
- A later ABC News report (March 2002) quoted an anonymous "former high-ranking American intelligence official" saying the FBI determined the men were conducting surveillance. The public narrative quickly entered the 9/11 conspiracy ecosystem and has since been invoked in fringe stories about Israeli advance knowledge.

## CJ Werleman video (Jan 13, 2025)
- I pulled the full transcript of the CJ Werleman episode titled “Finally, Evidence Tying Israel to 9/11 [Exposed]” and saved it as `transcript_raw.txt` in the workspace root. The clip rehashes the Urban Moving Systems story, alleges high-level Israeli knowledge of the attacks, and leans heavily on the same FBI reports cited above.
- The video’s new details add: (a) the five detainees spent roughly 71 days in federal custody, underwent repeated polygraphs, and were ultimately released after a deal arranged at the highest levels of the Bush administration; (b) one of the detainees (Omer Marmari) told investigators the attacks gave Israel “hope that the world will now understand us,” and called Americans “naïve”; (c) Benjamin Netanyahu allegedly said Israel “benefited” from 9/11 because it swung American public support in their favor; and (d) the detainees were quietly returned to Israel once Ashcroft signed off on their release.
- Fact-checking: (1) The New York Times reported in November 2001 that Kurzberg’s attorney understood Attorney General Ashcroft had to approve the detainees’ release, and follow-up coverage confirms the 71-day detention and polygraph-heavy interrogation before deportation.[^1] (2) The quote often attributed to Netanyahu (“We are benefiting …”) is documented by Foreign Policy and Democracy Now as coming from Ma’ariv’s coverage of a 2002 speech he gave while prime minister; the same article notes the speech discussed public opinion shifting in Israel’s favor after 9/11.[^2] (3) The FBI archive (section 5) contains the detainees’ own statements (and summarized interviews) where they describe viewing the attacks as “a positive development,” including the “hope” wording attributed to Marmari.[^3] (4) The CJ Werleman video does not produce new official documentation tying Israel to advance knowledge beyond these FOIA documents; the raw PDFs remain the best primary evidence for the claim, so treat the rest as interpretation layered on top of those scans.

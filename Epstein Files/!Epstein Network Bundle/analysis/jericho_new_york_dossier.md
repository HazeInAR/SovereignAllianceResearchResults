## Historical context that still matters
- **Quaker roots & abolition activism:** Jericho began as a Quaker farming community in 1648, renamed from Lusum in 1692 after the Biblical Jericho. The Quaker meeting house (1788) is still active, and abolitionists such as Elias Hicks used the area as a base for emancipation work and the Underground Railroad. The same sense of "hidden doors" and quiet sanctums that sheltered slaves centuries ago still exists in the disposition of Jericho estates and gated communities.
- **Infrastructure build-out:** Post-WWII developers leveled "Old Jericho" to widen Jericho Turnpike and built the schools, shopping, and water districts that now support modern espionage-friendly assets (hydrated power, multiple ZIP codes, and low-profile theatres/venues like the New York Community Bank Theatre that attract wealthy audiences while staying off the high-visibility radar).

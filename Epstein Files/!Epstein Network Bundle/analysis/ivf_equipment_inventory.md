# IVF Lab Equipment Inventory

## Primary equipment to look for in invoices and vendor records
- Incubators (standard & time-lapse/CO₂-equipped): the deck highlights temperature control, CO₂ regulation, HEPA filtration, humidity regulation, data logging, and real-time monitoring. Any invoices referencing CO₂ incubators, temperature chambers, or "environmental control" rigs should be tagged here.
- Centrifuges: rotor/motor combos plus centrifuge tubes. Watch for purchases marked "sperm prep," "density separation," or "RCF control" tied to the same financier wires as the cloning labs.
- Workstations/laminar flow benches: laminar HEPA air systems, ergonomic designs that integrate microscopes/incubators. Look for suppliers of "laminar flow hoods"/"clean benches" (especially in horse vs. human paths).
- Microscopes: inverted/polarized light units for oocyte/sperm/embryo assessment (ICSI, IVF observations). Any microscope line items should be cross-checked against the micromanipulator gear.
- Micromanipulators (primary & add-ons): joystick controls, micropipette holders, and imaging integration for ICSI/embryo biopsy/assisted hatching. Invoice descriptions mentioning "micromanipulator with imaging" or "IMSI platforms" are critical.
- Micropipettes: precision pipette sets for ICSI (look for references to "ICSI needles" or "microsyringe systems").
- Air handling units & QC gadgets: HEPA/UV purification, thermo-couplers, gas analyzers, VOC/CO₂ monitors, alarm integrations.
- Secondary gear: cryo-containers, refrigerators, water purification systems, gas analyzers, alarm systems, and laminar/air-handling accessories that support the main culture suite.
- Validation/test infrastructure: QC devices for temperature/gas/VOC, burning-in, sperm survival assays, microbiology sampling kits.

## Workflow/Quality Practices (context for invoices)
- The slides emphasize SOPs, risk management, and good laboratory practice standards, so invoices referencing "SOP compliance," "QC audits," or "GLP validated" equipment are more likely tied to the cloning/genetic infrastructure.
- ART level breakdown: invoices for microscopes, centrifuges, incubators (minimum two), laminar airflows, sperm counting chambers, cryopreservation sets, ovum aspiration pumps, USG machines, anesthesia/resuscitation gear, etc.

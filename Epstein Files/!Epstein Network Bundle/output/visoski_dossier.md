- DOJ files describe him overseeing properties, staffing, and even roadwork at Epstein’s Palm Beach, Virgin Islands, and Zorro Ranch compounds while reporting directly to Epstein on logistics.

## DOJ Proffer Follow-up — "Queen for a Day" Context
- **EFTA00159712 (FD-302, 02/09/2021):** The eight-page interview memo records Visoski being interviewed pursuant to a **proffer agreement** (colloquially "Queen for a Day") via video conference with his attorneys just before the Maxwell trial.
  - **Attendees:** Glen McGorty (defense counsel), AUSAs Danielle Giffuni & Daniel Zelenko, FBI Special Agents.
  - **Legal context:** Under a proffer agreement, Visoski had **full immunity from self-incrimination** for anything disclosed during the interview—prosecutors gave him a legal free pass to confess to his own crimes without exposure.
  - **The critical question:** **Whatever Visoski said (or didn't say) during that Feb. 2021 proffer is what he was willing to put on the record even when fully legally protected.** This reveals his true calculation:
    1. **Genuine ignorance theory:** He saw nothing in 1,000 flights (strains credibility given manifest control).
    2. **Strategic silence theory:** He knew exactly what not to say, suggesting he feared the network more than the law.
  - **Fear of the "club" > fear of prison:** Visoski had immunity to disclose everything about Epstein's operation, Maxwell's role, the oligarchs and bankers who flew, the victims' routes. His silence—even under legal protection—suggests he feared **retaliation from Epstein's network** (Maxwell still alive, estate still powerful, connected billionaires and foreign officials) more than federal prosecution.
  - The fact that prosecutors kept him on record just months before trial indicates they either valued his cooperation or wanted his official statement locked in for trial credibility.
